# Admin Workload & Separation Guide

This guide explains how to organize and divide placement work across multiple campuses, departments, and roles to ensure efficiency and clear accountability.

---

## 1. Geographical Separation (By Centre)
The first level of organization is by **Campus (Centre)**. Each centre (e.g., Bangalore, Noida, Pune) should operate like its own ecosystem while reporting to a central authority.

*   **Centre Admins:** These are admins who only see and manage students within their specific city.
*   **Why it works:** It prevents "data noise." A Noida admin doesn't need to know about a Bangalore student's profile status unless it's a cross-campus drive.
*   **Access Level:** Locked to only their city’s data.

## 2. Departmental Separation (By School/Vertical)
Even within one campus, the workload is often too large for one person. We divide this by **School or Department** (e.g., Technology, Management, Healthcare).

*   **School Leads:** An admin can be responsible for only "School of Technology" students.
*   **Why it works:** It allows the admin to become an expert in that specific industry. They know the technical requirements and the specific student pool better.
*   **Access Level:** Filtered by city AND department.

## 3. Functional Separation (By Task Type)
Instead of dividing by "who" (students), we can divide by "what" (tasks). This is great for large teams.

*   **A. Corporate Relations (CR) Admin:** Focuses entirely on Recruiters. They handle job postings, company onboarding, and recruiter verification.
*   **B. Student Success Admin:** Focuses on profile verification. They check if resumes are good, verify CGPAs, and handle student queries.
*   **C. Scheduling Admin:** Their only job is coordination. They manage the interview calendar and ensure interviewers and students are in the right place at the right time.

## 4. The "Super Admin" (The Orchestrator)
The **Super Admin** sits at the top and has "The God View."

*   **Role:** They don't handle daily tasks but monitor overall performance.
*   **Capabilities:**
    *   Compare placement rates between Bangalore and Noida.
    *   Reassign a student or recruiter to a different admin.
    *   Override decisions and handle high-level settings.

---

## Summary: How a Standard Workflow Looks

1.  **Job Comes In:** The **CR Admin** creates the job.
2.  **Filtering:** The system alerts relevant **Centre Admins** (e.g., this job is only for Bangalore).
3.  **Verification:** The **Student Success Admin** in Bangalore verifies that the applicants meet the criteria.
4.  **Interviewing:** The **Scheduling Admin** sets up the rounds.
5.  **Analytics:** The **Super Admin** views the final report to see if everything went smoothly.

---

### Is this separation needed?
**Absolutely.** Without it:
1.  Admins will see too much irrelevant data (Information Overload).
2.  Errors occur (A Noida admin accidentally rejecting a Bangalore student).
3.  No Accountability (Hard to tell which admin is falling behind or doing great work).
